## 📖 About EcoTrace

Most people know *climate change* is happening, but very few know exactly **what's happening in their own country** — how polluted their air really is, how scarce their water is becoming, or how much forest cover they're losing every year.

**EcoTrace** closes that gap. Enter your country, and instantly see a clear, prioritized breakdown of the real environmental challenges it faces — then take a concrete action, prove you did it, and get recognized for it publicly.

This isn't just an awareness app — it's a call to action with a built-in reward loop: **Know → Act → Prove → Share.**

---

## ✨ Features

- 🌐 **Country-wise Climate Report** — search any country and get a structured report covering Air Pollution, Water Scarcity, Deforestation, Extreme Heat, Plastic Pollution, and Biodiversity Loss
- 🚦 **Priority Tagging** — every issue is flagged `HIGH PRIORITY` or `MEDIUM` so users know what matters most
- 💪 **"How You Can Contribute" Action Cards** — Plant a Tree, Reduce Plastic, Save Water, Cycle Instead, Go Vegetarian, Save Energy, Buy Local, Spread Awareness
- 📸 **Proof of Action** — upload a photo or video showing the action you actually took
- 🎉 **Personalized Recognition Card** — submit your name + email and get a shareable card crediting your contribution
- 🔗 **One-Click Social Sharing** — share your recognition card directly to LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Instagram, or Facebook
- 🌗 **Light / Dark Theme Toggle**
- 🎨 **Smooth animated hero section** with a fully custom dark, eco-themed UI

## 🚀 How It Works

1. Enter your country's name (e.g. `India`)
2. View your **Climate Report** — a breakdown of the country's key environmental issues with priority levels
3. Pick an action under **"How You Can Contribute"**
4. Take that action in real life, then **upload a photo/video** as proof
5. Enter your **name and email**
6. Receive a **personalized Recognition Card**
7. **Share it** on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Instagram, or Facebook to inspire others

## 💻 Getting Started

This is a pure frontend project (HTML/CSS/JS) — no build tools required.


Then simply open `index.html` in your browser, or run it through a local dev server (e.g. the **Live Server** extension in VS Code) for the best experience.
